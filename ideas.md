# GMA Website Design Ideas

## Approach 1: Bauhaus Precision
<response>
<text>
**Design Movement**: Bauhaus / Swiss International Typographic Style
**Core Principles**:
- Grid-based asymmetry with strong vertical rhythm
- Form follows function — every element earns its place
- Monochromatic base with a single gold accent
- Generous negative space as a luxury signal

**Color Philosophy**: Near-black (#0A0A0A) background for sections, pure white text, warm gold (#C9A96E) as the sole accent. The restraint communicates confidence.

**Layout Paradigm**: Full-bleed sections with offset text blocks. Hero uses a diagonal split — text left, abstract geometric right. Navigation is minimal top-bar with logo left, links right.

**Signature Elements**:
- Thin horizontal rules as section dividers
- Large numerals (01, 02, 03) as section markers
- Geometric line art as background texture

**Interaction Philosophy**: Hover reveals — content appears on hover with opacity transitions. Buttons use border-only style until hovered.

**Animation**: Staggered fade-up on scroll entry. Smooth 0.4s ease transitions. No bouncing or spring physics.

**Typography System**: Playfair Display (headings, display) + DM Sans (body). Tight letter-spacing on headings, generous line-height on body.
</text>

## Approach 2: Editorial Luxury (CHOSEN)
<response>
<text>
**Design Movement**: Contemporary Luxury Editorial — inspired by high-end financial and consulting brands (McKinsey, Goldman Sachs, Lazard)

**Core Principles**:
- Stark white canvas with deep charcoal text
- Typographic hierarchy as the primary visual tool
- Minimal color — charcoal, white, warm off-white, and a single deep navy accent
- Asymmetric column layouts that feel editorial, not corporate-generic

**Color Philosophy**: Background #FAFAF8 (warm white), text #1A1A1A (near-black), accent #1B3A5C (deep navy), secondary accent #8B7355 (warm bronze). This palette signals authority and global reach without coldness.

**Layout Paradigm**: Offset two-column layouts. Hero section uses full-width with large display type on left, subtle geometric pattern on right. Sections alternate between text-heavy and visual-heavy columns.

**Signature Elements**:
- Thin vertical lines as column separators
- Oversized section numbers in light gray
- Subtle dot-grid or line-grid background texture on select sections

**Interaction Philosophy**: Understated hover states — underline animations on links, subtle background shifts on cards. Everything feels deliberate.

**Animation**: Fade-in-up on scroll (Framer Motion). Staggered children. Parallax on hero background. No excessive motion.

**Typography System**: Cormorant Garamond (display headings — elegant, authoritative) + Outfit (body and UI — clean, modern). This contrast creates visual tension that feels premium.
</text>

## Approach 3: Minimalist Monochrome
<response>
<text>
**Design Movement**: Japanese Minimalism meets New York Corporate
**Core Principles**:
- Pure black and white with maximum contrast
- Silence as design — vast empty sections
- Typography IS the design
- One red accent (#C0392B) for critical CTAs only

**Color Philosophy**: #000000 and #FFFFFF only, with #C0392B for one accent. Extreme restraint.

**Layout Paradigm**: Full-width text blocks. No cards, no borders. Just type on white or white on black.

**Signature Elements**:
- Full-bleed black sections alternating with white
- Massive display type (120px+)
- Zero decorative elements

**Interaction Philosophy**: Cursor changes on hover. Magnetic button effects. Everything is interaction-driven.

**Animation**: Clip-path reveals. Text scramble effects. Bold and theatrical.

**Typography System**: Space Grotesk (all text, varying weights). Monospace accents for data/numbers.
</text>

## SELECTED: Approach 2 — Editorial Luxury

Rationale: Best matches "高端、简洁、国际化" (high-end, clean, international) brief. The editorial luxury style is used by top-tier consulting and financial firms globally, communicating authority, trust, and global reach — exactly what GMA needs.
